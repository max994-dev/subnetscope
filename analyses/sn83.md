# Subnet 83 — CliqueAI (Graph Optimization)

> Analyzed: 2026-05-05 · easy-entry score at time of analysis: 52.6/100

## TL;DR

CliqueAI is a **combinatorial optimization subnet** — miners solve NP-hard
graph problems (maximum clique, graph coloring, network flow). No GPU
required; the task is pure algorithmic / AI search. Burn fee is mid-tier
(~0.30 τ), 246 active miners means competition is dense. The reward system
explicitly values **diversity of approach** alongside quality — you don't have
to have the best solution globally, you need a different one.

## What it actually does

Validators generate hard graph instances (large random graphs, structured
graphs) and challenge miners to find:

- **Maximum clique** — find the largest complete sub-graph
- **Graph coloring** — assign colors to nodes so no two adjacent nodes share
  a color, minimizing the number of colors used
- **Network flow** — find optimal flow through a weighted directed graph

These are all NP-hard — there's no known polynomial-time algorithm.
Heuristics (simulated annealing, genetic algorithms, branch-and-bound) are
the practical approach. The scoring system uses a **dual-metric**:

1. **Solution quality** — how good is your answer vs the theoretical optimum?
2. **Algorithmic diversity** — does your approach differ from what other
   miners are submitting?

Miners with moderate quality but unique approaches earn more than those
replicating the top miner's algorithm. This prevents winner-take-all
consolidation.

## Reward structure

Miners are ranked by composite (quality × diversity) and emissions are
distributed proportionally via exponential moving average. Better solutions
earn more, but uniqueness is also rewarded — a good novel heuristic earns
more than a copied great one.

## What miners run

- **Hardware:** CPU only (heuristic search is CPU-bound). More cores = wider
  beam search = better solutions in the time window.
- **Stack:** Python or any language; the axon receives a graph definition and
  returns a solution. Competitive miners use custom C++/Rust search kernels.
- **Research:** Familiarity with algorithms like Simulated Annealing, Tabu
  Search, GRASP, or ML-guided branch-and-bound helps.

## Honest assessment

**Pros:**
- No GPU required
- Diversity reward prevents total winner-take-all
- Interesting intellectual problem — combinatorial optimization has real
  academic depth
- Burn fee moderate, not extreme

**Cons:**
- 246 miners (very crowded)
- 0.30 τ burn fee means you need proven earnings before registering
- Requires algorithms knowledge — this is harder than "run a model on HuggingFace"
- Low development activity (3 commits in 30 days) — risk of abandonment

5. Make your approach genuinely different from the obvious implementation to
   maximize diversity score
