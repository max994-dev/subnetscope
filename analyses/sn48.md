# Subnet 48 — Quantum Compute (qBitTensor)

> Analyzed: 2026-05-05 · easy-entry score at time of analysis: 48.0/100

## TL;DR

qBitTensor's quantum compute subnet is genuinely novel — miners run **real
quantum circuits** on physical QPUs (quantum processors) or high-performance
quantum simulators. Near-zero burn fee (~0.0005 τ), but only **6 active
miners** with 0 slots free. You need access to an actual quantum computer or
partnership with a QPU provider. In 2026 they added private execution for
enterprise customers. Extremely niche, extremely high barrier.

## What it actually does

Users submit quantum circuits in OpenQASM 2.0/3.0 format. Miners execute them
on:
- **Physical QPUs** (e.g., IBM Quantum, IonQ, Rigetti, Quantinuum) via API
  partnerships, or
- **HPC quantum simulators** (classical hardware that simulates quantum systems
  up to ~50 qubits at extreme cost)

Validators score miners on:
- Circuit execution fidelity (results match expected quantum distribution)
- Latency (time-to-result)
- Availability (uptime during the validation window)

In 2026 a **two-tier credit system** was introduced:
- **Spark Credits** (free): public circuit execution via lottery allocation
- **Paid Credits**: private execution with data isolation for enterprise

## Reward structure

Standard Bittensor proportional emission based on validator scores. With only
6 active miners, the per-miner share is very high. The 2026 bounty model
rewards miners whose results contribute to meaningful scientific improvements.

## What miners run

- **Required:** Access to a physical QPU (IBM/IonQ/etc. API keys, often
  paid/waitlisted), or a classical HPC system capable of quantum simulation.
- **Software:** Python, the `qbittensor-labs/quantum-compute` repo,
  OpenQASM tools.
- **No consumer GPU path** — this is not a "plug in your gaming GPU" subnet.

## Realistic earnings

| Setup | TAO/day (est.) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Access to 1× IBM Quantum free tier | ~0.5–2 τ | limited job quota |
| Partnership with QPU provider | ~2–5 τ | if you have sustained access |

With only 6 active miners and decent QPU access, earnings can be exceptional.
But the bottleneck is hardware access, not algorithms.

## Honest assessment

**Pros:**
- Only 6 active miners → extremely high per-miner emission
- Near-zero burn fee
- Genuinely novel tech; qBitTensor is publishing formal arXiv research
- Enterprise revenue path with paid credits in 2026

**Cons:**
- Requires actual quantum hardware or QPU API access (not consumer-accessible)
- Quantum hardware is expensive and waitlisted
- Classical simulation is energy-intensive and memory-limited
- Very niche; low liquidity and market cap
