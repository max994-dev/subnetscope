# Subnet 54 — Yanez MIID (Identity Data)

> Analyzed: 2026-05-05 · easy-entry score at time of analysis: 53.2/100

## TL;DR

Yanez MIID generates **synthetic adversarial identity data** — name variations,
transliterations, DOB/address permutations, and face image variations — used
to stress-test financial crime detection systems (KYC, sanctions screening).
Burn fee is effectively free (~0.0005 τ), no GPU needed for the text tasks.
Niche product with a real commercial use case, but limited community info and
188 active miners means competition is real.

## What it actually does

Financial compliance systems fail when sanctions names are transliterated
differently (Mukhammad vs Mohammed vs Muhammad). MIID trains and tests those
systems by generating controlled adversarial identity variations. Validators
issue challenges like:

- "Generate 50 variations of this name across Arabic, Russian, and Chinese
  scripts"
- "Produce face images of this seed person under different lighting/angles"
- "Create address variations for this entity that would evade string-match
  screening"

Miners return structured datasets. Validators score on:
- **Accuracy** — do variations conform to known transliteration standards?
- **Novelty** — are the variations genuinely diverse (not copy-pastes)?
- **Adversarial value** — would these trick a real sanctions screener?

Future planned tasks: biometric data generation, document synthesis.

## Reward structure

Proportional to score — no winner-take-all. Validators rank miners and
assign emissions proportionally to performance. 188 active miners split
~36 τ/day, similar density to sn13 or sn72.

## What miners run

- **Hardware:** No GPU for text tasks. Face image generation may benefit from
  a small GPU (4 GB VRAM enough).
- **Stack:** Python, the Yanez repo, text generation models (LLMs fine-tuned
  for name transliteration), optionally a face-synthesis model.
- **Competitive edge:** Deep knowledge of multi-script transliteration or
  identity compliance domain.

## Honest assessment

**Pros:**
- Near-zero burn fee (~0.0005 τ) — cheapest registration after sn13/sn64
- No GPU for text tasks
- Real commercial product (compliance fintech has budget)
- Not oversaturated with GPU-farm miners

**Cons:**
- 188 miners is crowded
- Niche domain — hard to build competitive models without compliance expertise
- Limited public info / community vs sn13 or sn72
- Face-image tasks require a GPU eventually
